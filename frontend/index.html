Strategy Overview

The vol direction model (LSTM classifier) predicts whether short-term realized volatility will increase the next day. When rising volatility is detected, portfolio exposure is reduced. When volatility is calm, exposure is increased.

This implements volatility-targeted position sizing — a standard systematic risk management technique. The strategy is always long (no shorting). Leverage is capped at 1.5×.

Signal: vol_prob > 0.6 → reduce exposure (0.5–0.9×) · vol_prob < 0.4 → increase exposure (1.1–1.5×) · else hold. Positions smoothed with 5-day EMA. Transaction cost 0.02% per unit position change.

Vol model F1 on S&P 500 test set: 0.645 — beats persistence baseline. Transfer learning: S&P 500 parent → ticker child.
